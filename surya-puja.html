Surya Puja: Worship of the Sun God

Surya Puja is a sacred Hindu ritual dedicated to the worship of Surya, the Sun God, believed to bring spiritual growth, health, prosperity, and vitality.

What is Surya Puja?

Surya Puja is a sacred Hindu ritual dedicated to the worship of Surya, the Sun God. Surya is considered the symbol of power, health, vitality, and wealth in Hinduism. Worshipping Surya is believed to invoke divine protection, positivity, and blessings for a prosperous life.

How to Perform Surya Puja (Step-by-Step)

Surya Puja is best performed early in the morning during sunrise, between 5:30 AM and 6:30 AM. Here's a step-by-step guide:

  1. Preparation: Set up a clean place, gather required items like water, flowers, incense, and an idol or photo of Lord Surya.
  2. Meditation: Sit facing the sun and calm your mind before starting the ritual.
  3. Offer Water (Arghya): Hold a vessel of water, chant the Surya mantra, and offer the water to the Sun.
  4. Chant Surya Mantras: Recite mantras such as "Om Surya Namaha" and the Gayatri Mantra 108 times using prayer beads (mala).
  5. Offer Flowers and Incense: Offer fresh flowers and light incense while continuing your prayers.
  6. Perform Prayers: Close your eyes and pray for health, prosperity, and success.
  7. Conclude the Puja: Bow down to Lord Surya and perform an Aarti (light a lamp and move it in a circular motion).
  8. Prasad (Offering): Consume or distribute prasad (such as fruits or sweets) after completing the ritual.
  9. Consistency: Perform Surya Puja regularly, preferably every day or on Sundays.

Conclusion

Surya Puja is a deeply spiritual practice in Hinduism that brings numerous benefits, including physical healing, spiritual growth, wealth, and prosperity. By performing Surya Puja regularly, you can connect with the divine energy of the Sun and invite positive changes into your life.
